About Me
Applied statistician with significant scientific computing experience. Currently working on a number of problems at the intersection of biology, computer science, and statistics. Main research focus involves developing novel statistical methods for analyzing data arising from multiple high-throughput 'omics platforms. Also keen on researching, developing, and integrating project management frameworks to enhance bioinformatics analyst productivity and facilitate project reproducibility. In a former role as lead statistician in a high-throughput genomics lab, I was tasked with designing pipelines for conducting genome-wide association studies (GWAS) and implementing population genetics analyses using high-density SNP genotyping data. Additionally, I have significant experience developing, comparing and validating workflows for the analysis of whole-genome and whole-transcriptome next-generation sequencing data.
